# How to Make the Most of One-on-Ones / 如何充分利用一对一

> One-on-ones with your manager are one of the most valuable regular investments in your career. This guide describes what each level expects from this relationship, and what both sides should work on together.
> Applicable to the IC track, the strategic leader track, and roles that span both.

> 与经理的一对一是职业生涯中最有价值的定期投入之一。本指南描述各级别对这段关系的期待，以及双方需要共同努力的方向。
> 适用于 IC 路径和战略领导者路径，以及跨越两条路径的角色。

## How the Relationship Evolves / 关系的演变

| Stage / 阶段 | Characteristics / 特征 |
|------|------|
| **Early levels (L1-L2) / 早期级别（L1–L2）** | Manager is deeply involved in daily work, provides direct guidance / 经理深度参与日常工作，提供直接指导 |
| **Mid-level (L3-L4) / 中级（L3–L4）** | Shifts to a thought partnership; manager provides context and trade-offs / 转向思维伙伴关系，经理提供背景和权衡 |
| **Senior levels (L5+) / 高级别（L5+）** | Approaches an equal partnership, primarily strategic alignment / 趋近平等伙伴关系，战略对齐为主 |

For ICs, "your work" at senior levels expands to uplifting those around you, shaping technical direction, and creating leverage across the team.
For strategic leaders, it also includes team health, people decisions, and organizational dynamics.

> 对于 IC，"你的工作"在高级别会扩展到提升周围的人、塑造技术方向、在团队中创造杠杆效应。
> 对于战略领导者，还包括团队健康、人员决策和组织动态。

## Best Practices / 最佳实践

### Talk About Career Development / 谈论职业发展

Don't wait for the formal performance review cycle. Share your directional goals, the skills you're developing, and the opportunities that could accelerate your growth. Your manager can only advocate for you and create the right opportunities if they know your goals. Revisit regularly — goals evolve.

> 不要等到正式绩效评估周期。分享目标方向、正在培养的技能、能加速发展的机会。经理只有了解你的目标，才能为你发声并创造合适的机会。定期回顾——目标会不断演变。

### Be Honest About Your State / 如实反映状态

Not just project progress, but personal state too. Burnout, loss of motivation, feeling lost about direction — these are all topics for one-on-ones. A good manager can help you course-correct and re-prioritize, but only if they know what's going on.

> 不只是项目进展，也包括个人状态。精力透支、失去动力、对方向感到迷茫——这些都是一对一该提的话题。好的经理可以帮你纠偏、重新排序优先级，但前提是他们知道情况。

### Own the Agenda / 掌控议程

The one-on-one is **your time**. Come with prepared topics; don't wait for your manager to drive. Recommended tool: a continuously updated shared document with your manager where you jot down topics as they come up during the week. Important things won't be forgotten, and meetings won't devolve into status reports.

> 一对一是**你的时间**。带着准备好的话题来，不要等经理主导。推荐工具：与经理共享的持续更新文档，随时记录一周内浮现的话题，重要的事不会被遗忘，会议也不会沦为进度汇报。

### Don't Let It Become a Status Update / 不要让它变成状态更新会

Status information should be communicated asynchronously in writing (Slack, tickets, shared docs). If your manager needs to ask "what have you been working on," that's a signal to improve async communication. Save face-to-face time for things that truly need dialogue: **decisions, trade-offs, feedback, direction, and development**.

> 状态信息应通过书面形式传达（Slack、工单、共享文档）。如果经理需要问你"最近在做什么"，这是改善异步沟通的信号。面对面时间留给真正需要对话的事：**决策、权衡、反馈、方向和发展**。

### Keep Your Manager Informed / 让经理保持知情

Surface blockers, risks, and important decisions early — ideally before they become problems, with a suggested resolution path. Key principle: **your manager should never hear about something important regarding you for the first time from someone else**. This isn't over-reporting; it's building the trust that earns you more autonomy.

> 尽早提出阻碍、风险和重要决策——最好在演变成问题之前，附上建议的解决路径。关键原则：**经理永远不应该从别人那里第一次听到关于你的重要事项**。这不是过度汇报，而是建立能赢得更多自主权的信任。

### Give Feedback, Not Just Receive It / 给予反馈，而不只是接受反馈

The one-on-one is a two-way relationship. If something isn't working — the way support is given, clarity of direction, the types of opportunities — say so. Express it constructively and specifically. Most managers want this feedback and can't improve without it.

> 一对一是双向关系。如果某些事情不奏效——支持方式、方向清晰度、机会类型——请说出来。以建设性、具体的方式表达。大多数经理希望得到这样的反馈，没有它就无法改进。

### Follow Through / 言出必行

What you commit to in a one-on-one (updates, conversations, work), do it — or explicitly bring it back for discussion. Consistency between words and actions is how trust accumulates over time.

> 在一对一中承诺的事（更新、对话、工作），就去做，或者明确重新提出讨论。言行一致是信任随时间积累的方式。

## Level-by-Level Guide / 各级别指南

### L1

**Core topics: Learning and adaptation / 核心主题：学习与适应**

- Questions about the work / 关于工作的问题
- Feedback on recent deliverables / 近期交付成果的反馈
- Discussions about "what good looks like" / "什么是好"的讨论

The manager will typically supplement the agenda and give direct guidance — this is appropriate at this stage and not a sign of distrust. Come with **specific questions and things you're unsure about**; the clearer you are about where you're stuck, the more valuable the conversation.

> 经理通常会补充议程并给出直接指导——这在此阶段是合适的，并非不信任。带着**具体问题和不确定的事情**来参会；越清晰地表达卡在哪里，对话就越有价值。

---

### L2

**Core topics: Your growth, not your work itself / 核心主题：你的成长，而非你的工作本身**

- Post-mortems on deliverables and improvement points / 交付成果的复盘和改进点
- Skills you're developing / 正在培养的技能
- Feedback you've received / 收到的反馈
- Early conversations about what the next level looks like / 关于下一级别是什么样子的早期对话

The manager is more **coaching than directing**. Come with your own perspective and questions, not just status reports. Start discussing what types of problems and opportunities energize you.

> 经理更多是在**辅导而非指挥**。带着自己的观点和问题来，而不只是状态汇报。开始谈什么类型的问题和机会让你充满动力。

---

### L3

**Core topics: Thought partnership / 核心主题：思维伙伴关系**

- Difficult problems you want to think through / 希望梳理的难题
- Priority decisions you want input on / 希望获得意见的优先级决策
- Cross-functional dynamics you need to navigate / 需要应对的跨职能动态
- Progress toward the next level / 向下一级别发展的过程
- **(Strategic leaders)** People management challenges: how to develop a direct report, handle tough performance situations, think through hiring decisions / **（战略领导者）** 人员管理挑战：如何培养某位直接下属、处理棘手的绩效情况、考虑招聘决策

Come with **your perspective**, not just problems — your manager wants to react to your thinking, not fill in a blank page.

> 带着**你的观点**来，而不只是问题——经理希望对你的思考作出回应，而不是填补一张白纸。

---

### L4

**Core topics: Strategic alignment / 核心主题：战略对齐**

- The state of your area / 你所在领域的现状
- Decisions you've made and why / 做出的决策及其原因
- Where you need organizational support / 需要组织层面支持的地方
- Your read on team health and stakeholder dynamics / 对团队健康和干系人动态的判断
- **(Strategic leaders)** Team composition, direct report development, people situation support / **（战略领导者）** 团队构成、直接下属发展、人员情况支持

Your manager relies on you to manage the day-to-day; one-on-ones are where they help you integrate strategic context and create organizational visibility. Share **your read on the big picture**, not a list of requests.

> 经理依赖你管理日常事务；一对一是他们帮你整合战略背景、创造组织能见度的场合。分享**对全局的判断**，而非一份请求清单。

> **Note:** At this level, feedback starts to change — it's no longer direct instructions but rather a question, an emphasis on an angle, or a connection being drawn. This is intentional; pay attention to what your manager is directing your focus toward.

> **注意：** 这个级别，反馈开始变化——不再是直接指令，更多是一个问题、一个角度的强调，或一个联系的建立。这是有意为之的，留意经理在引导你关注什么。

---

### L5

**Core topics: Direction, health, and personal growth / 核心主题：方向、健康状况与个人成长**

- Priority-setting across multiple competing initiatives / 跨多个竞争性项目的优先级排序
- Decisions that require organizational context to move forward / 需要组织背景才能推进的决策
- Feedback about how to operate at this scope / 关于在这个范围内如何运作的反馈

Your manager is still an important source of direction — weighing in on priorities, sharing context, giving direct and specific feedback. Come with **your own proposals and perspectives**; the best conversations are genuine exchanges of ideas. Feedback becomes less explicit, more focused on the habits and patterns that determine effectiveness.

> 经理仍是方向的重要来源——就优先级发表意见、分享背景、给出直接而具体的反馈。带着**你自己的方案和观点**来；最好的对话是真正的思想交流。反馈变得不那么明确，更聚焦于决定效能的习惯和模式。

> **On energy investment:** Tactical and operational topics are fine, but if they consistently dominate the conversation, reflect: does the team have the capacity to take on more of the day-to-day? Could staffing and organizational structure free up time for higher-leverage work? These are organizational questions that leaders at this level should proactively think about.

> **关于精力投入：** 战术性和运营性话题没问题，但如果它们持续主导对话，需反思：团队是否具备能力承担更多日常事务？人员配置和组织结构是否能解放时间专注于高杠杆工作？这些是这个级别领导者应主动思考的组织性问题。

---

### L6

**Core topics: Direction and health of the team or domain / 核心主题：团队或领域的方向与健康状况**

- How the people you're developing are growing / 你在培养的人是如何成长的
- Cross-functional dynamics that need attention / 需要关注的跨职能动态
- Prioritizing the most important work among competing demands / 在竞争性需求中对最重要的工作排序

Your manager provides less direct instruction than at L5 — more organizational context, feedback on operating style, and help thinking through the most important decisions and trade-offs. Come with **your own analysis and recommendations**; your manager is more likely testing your reasoning than giving direction. Feedback becomes more targeted — fewer things, but each carries more weight.

> 经理提供的直接指令少于 L5——更多是组织背景、运作方式反馈、帮你思考最重要的决策和权衡。带着**你自己的分析和建议**来；经理更可能是在检验你的思路，而不是给方向。反馈更有针对性——事情更少，但每件分量更重。

---

### L7

**Core topics: High-leverage strategic forum / 核心主题：高杠杆战略论坛**

- Company-level strategy you're shaping or influencing / 正在塑造或影响的公司级战略
- The direction and ambition of your domain / 你所在领域的方向与雄心
- How your work connects to the broader business trajectory / 你的工作如何与更广泛的业务轨迹相连
- The quality of leadership within your scope / 你所管辖范围内的领导力质量

Your manager almost never tells you what to do — they provide strategic context, help you navigate decisions with company-wide impact, and ensure your work creates maximum impact. Feedback tends to be **indirect**: reframing, questions about stakeholders, prompts to take a longer view.

> 经理几乎不告诉你该做什么——提供战略背景，帮你应对具有全公司影响的决策，确保工作产生最大影响。反馈往往是**间接的**：重新框架、关于干系人的问题、引导放长眼光的提示。

---

### L8

**Core topics: Strategic partnership / 核心主题：战略伙伴关系**

- The most important strategic decisions / 最重要的战略决策
- The long-term trajectory of your domain or organization / 你所在领域或组织的长期走向
- How the entire division is positioned internally and externally / 整个部门在内部和外部的定位

Your manager rarely tells you what to do directly. Instead, they probe, raise unconsidered trade-offs, open doors to conversations or perspectives, and sharpen your thinking. This is the feedback style at this level: **less direction, more influence**.

> 经理很少直接告诉你该做什么。相反，他们追问、提出未权衡的考量、打开通往对话或视角的大门，锐化你的思维。这就是这个级别的反馈风格：**少指令，多影响**。
